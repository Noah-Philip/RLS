Understand the Issue

Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS) is a neurological condition that creates an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, often with uncomfortable sensations. Symptoms can intensify at night, disrupting sleep and daily functioning.

RLS affects people of all ages, with higher prevalence among women, older adults, and people managing chronic conditions. It matters because untreated RLS can lead to insomnia, anxiety, and diminished quality of life for patients and caregivers.

  • What is it? A sensory-motor disorder that disrupts rest and sleep.
  • Why does it matter? Sleep loss and pain can impact work, school, and mental health.
  • Who does it affect? An estimated 7-10% of adults, including children and pregnant people.

Data & Stories

  • Limited provider education on diagnosis and long-term care.
  • Few community resources for rural and underserved patients.

Know the Policy Landscape

Legislative Task

Support dedicated NIH funding for Restless Legs Syndrome research and improve clinician training.

Why legislators should care: Better treatment reduces long-term healthcare costs, improves sleep-related public health, and supports caregivers.

Symptoms & Diagnosis

Uncomfortable sensations appear at rest and improve with movement.
Symptoms intensify at night, leading to insomnia and daytime fatigue.
Providers review medical history, medications, iron levels, and sleep patterns.
Screening Clinical evaluation Treatment planning

Treatment & Care

Medications

Evidence-based prescriptions to reduce symptoms and improve sleep.

Lifestyle changes

Movement, hydration, and balanced nutrition to reduce flare-ups.

Sleep hygiene

Consistent bedtime routines, light management, and stress reduction.

Medical support

Work with specialists in neurology or sleep medicine.
